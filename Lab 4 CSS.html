Excerpt from The Song of Achilles

By Madeline Miller


"I listened and did not speak. Achilles' eyes were bright in the firelight, his face drawn sharply by the flickering shadows. I would know it in the dark or disguise, I told myself. I would know it even in madness" (Miller 84).



"We were like gods at the dawning of the world, and our joy was so bright we could see nothing else but the other" (Miller 103).
